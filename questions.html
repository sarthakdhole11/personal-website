here are some questions i keep returning to.

questions i love

  1. 1. What’s the ideal soup-to-noodle ratio when making cup noodles, maximizing the amount of soup that you get while ensuring flavorful and non-watery noodles?
  2. 2.
    important meet-ups
    What if Wittgenstein had not barged into Russell's office at Cambridge?
    What if Lennon hadn't bought that large Cadbury chocolate bar, which he was compelled to share with McCartney when they first met?
    What if Ramanujan’s letter had gone unopened or worse, dismissed as crankery of some overworked clerk?
    What if Kafka had just burned the manuscripts himself?
    What if Satyajit Ray had stayed in advertising?
    What if Vries and others hadn't rediscovered Mendel?
  3. 8. What if someone hadn't figured out a way to have the New Year's eye glasses design adjusted for the years after the 2000s ended? Would we not have those cool glasses anymore? Wouldn't that be a cultural loss of significance for all of us?
  4. 9. Why are some scientific or engineering problems more “beautiful” than others?
  5. 10. Why do philosophies associated with cultures separated both spatially and temporally repeatedly rediscover the same problems under new vocabularies?
  6. 11. Why do moral judgements feel intuitive and immediate while moral reasoning under any kind of framework feels artificial?
  7. 12. Why does mathematics developed with no application in mind often become indispensable later?
  8. 13. Why turbulence?
  9. 14. Lotka–Volterra modelling for animal behavior (Ethology) and Cournot modelling for firms in a financial market are fundamentally similar. So, is science even useful if it's so reductive and skips on the nuance?
  10. 15. How similar is the behaviour of animals and financial markets, really?
  11. 16. Why do people tend to respect the scientific method(s) considerably more than the legal method? Why are lawyers framed as demagogues while scientists are framed as truth-seekers, when both methods aim to reconstruct reality?
  12. 17. Why did the Beatles break up? Was it really Yoko Ono, or is that theory shaped by anti-Japanese propaganda of the time?
  13. 18. Were shamans of olden times schizotypic?
  14. 19. Why do some memes age rapidly while others stick around longer?
  15. 20. Is cultural preservation a worthwhile cause if cultures come and go as they do? If yes, are memes a good way to do so?
  16. 21. How does one acquire good taste?
  17. 22. How much does the medium actually change the message?
  18. 23. Are Wittgenstein’s and Nietzsche’s aphorisms some sort of philosophical method?
  19. 24. Why does Ex-Yugoslav New Wave/Rock repeatedly refer to the distinction between the East and the West and parody Western songs?
  20. 25. How real is Transcendental Meditation, and why do we value it more because of bands than empirical evidence?
  21. 26. To what extent is culture just a product of political and socioeconomic conditions?
  22. 27. Why am I more likely to trust an Overleaf (LaTeX) PDF than a Google Doc?
  23. 28. Why do babies actually cry? Is there an existential lesson here?
  24. 29. How can the philosophy of an architecture school translate into music or other media? Can two things belong to the same movement without sharing core characteristics?
  25. 30. In science, can theories belong to the same Lakatosian research program if they contradict earlier results while advancing the project?

the order here is irrelevant.
don't discriminate while thinking about them. try to record the thoughts you have as you think about these questions & reach out if youd like to discuss.
